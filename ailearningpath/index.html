for new technologies, the first part of a learning curve is the hardest because there is no obvious to start. However once you have some initial concepts to step up from, the curve become less steep.

This article/site describes a learning path for becoming acquainted with some intitial concepts of AI{Artificial Intelligence}. THis is the learning path I am taking and hope it will help you.

What you can gain from this is first, to have some insight and perspective on the readings and discussions you encounter on AI. second, to see how AI is starting to affect your life and the impact it will increasingly have on your work place and the world around you. You will gain some perspective on the concerns about AI as well as the promise for the good it can do.

you need to understand is that AI is a big idea, in fact it is many big ideas. And so, it is not something you will be able fully grasp in one brief article.

This path consists of a reading list, seveeral video presentations, tutorials and suggested courses to take for furthe study. I will present these resources in levels, using a order that builds understanding do a short review of each of those resources

There are several good tutorials on how to use each tool. I will point to and review these tutorials.

Point out difference with search tools. The it really is a natural language processor. Hence the name LLM or large language model.

The scope of this article is just to get you over the hump of the early learning curve. At the end, point to some limits or problem you might have with it. And point to some solutions that can be developed in the next generation of language models.

The start is to highlight the three big readily available chat tools: ChatGPT, Google Bard and Bing chat. I also want to bring in the open source AI suite from Hugging chat when I can deliver enough info so the reader can appreciate how they are different from the big three. and show how they add to the mix. They are more a part of stage two.

So I am thinking the way to present this is in stages or I could say levels. First you get your feet under you as to what AI is and why you should care. Then we go to the next level of understanding this big idea.
